# South Africa Species Explorer — how to use it

A pocket field guide for the Cal Academy South Africa trip (20 Jul – 1 Aug 2026). One
organism per row, every site and season, reconciled on the GBIF Backbone with eBird names
for birds. **It works fully offline once installed** — built for the plane and the field.

**Open it:** <https://ddkapan.github.io/saexplore/>

---

## 1. Put it on your devices — do this on wifi *before* you fly

The app caches itself the first time you open it online. After that it runs with no signal.

**On your iPhone (Safari):**
1. Open the link above in **Safari**.
2. Tap the **Share** button → **Add to Home Screen** → **Add**.
3. Open it from the new home-screen icon (it runs full-screen, like an app).
4. Look for the small **"Save photos for offline"** pill (bottom-right). Tap it and let it
   finish — it downloads the ~1,300 species photos. When it says **"Saved for offline ●"**
   you're set. (Text + data are already saved the moment the page loads; this step just adds
   the photos.)

**On your laptop (Chrome or Edge):**
1. Open the link. In the address bar you'll see an **Install** icon (or menu → *Install
   this site as an app*). Installing gives you a clean app window — optional but nice.
2. Or just leave it open as a tab. Either way, once it has loaded online it works offline.
3. Tap the same **"Save photos for offline"** pill to cache photos.

> **To confirm you're ready:** turn on Airplane Mode and reopen the app. The species list,
> search, photos, and journal should all still work. If they do, you're good for the flight.
> (No iPad needed — the laptop covers the big-screen reading.)

## 2. The layout — read down the page

The home screen is a short "funnel" you read top to bottom. Each numbered section can be
**collapsed** (tap its ▾ heading) once you've read it, so the working list rises to fill the
screen.

- **01 South Africa / 02 The two regions / 03 The trip** — the frame: Cape winter vs Lowveld
  dry season, the itinerary, and the shared map.
- **04 The sites** — the ten localities. Tap a site to focus it (map zooms, its field account
  opens, the list narrows to that column). Tap again to unfocus.
- **05 The groups** — the taxonomic groups (Birds, Mammals, Plants…).
- **06 Filters & evidence** — abundance, museum-specimen year, season, sort, and what the
  evidence glyphs mean.
- **07 The results** — the working checklist. This is where you'll spend your time.
- **08 Field journal** — your Grinnell journal, saved per day.

---

## 3. Finding things — "Filters at hand" (section 07)

A sticky strip sits above the list with everything you need:

- **Search (top row)** — the fastest tool. Type **any** name and it matches: common name,
  scientific name, an **eBird code** (e.g. `blkspa`… try `blagos1`), an old/synonym name, or a
  **local name in any of 11 South African languages** (Afrikaans, isiZulu, isiXhosa, Setswana,
  Xitsonga, siSwati, Sesotho, Sepedi, isiNdebele…). Searching *kolgans* finds the Egyptian
  Goose; *isibululu* finds the Puff Adder.
- **Groups row** — `all` / `none` on the left, then tap groups to show/hide them.
- **Sites row** — `all` shows every site; tap a site chip to focus just that column.
- **★ late Jul** — narrows to what's actually around in the trip window.

The count on the right shows how many of the 2,780 organisms match. Filters **stack** (they
narrow together) and never hide a name permanently — clear them to see everything again.

Deeper filters live in **section 06**: abundance (rare → common; none = all), specimen year,
season by quarter, and sort order.

## 4. A species' page (tap any row)

- **Photo** (CC-licensed via iNaturalist) and the classification.
- **Also known as** — every other name: other scientific names, alternate English names, and
  **local names by language**. (Shannon's books each use different names — they all resolve
  here.)
- **Mark it ★ focal** (your interest) or **⚑ tour** (to share) — marked species pin to the top
  of the list and lead the tour.
- **Where on this trip** — which of the ten sites, and the season sparkline.
- **Normally** — a short natural-history note.
- **Explore** — GBIF · iNaturalist · **eBird** (for birds, the eBird species page — the join
  key for your checklists) · Wikipedia.
- **Seen on this trip** — tap a site chip to log a sighting.

---

## 5. Lists — your picks, and the curated ones

A **list** is just a named set of species. That is the one idea to hold on to.

- Tap the **☆** beside any species to cycle **★ focal → ⚑ tour → off**. Those are simply two
  lists you always have.
- **The site specials are lists too** — Big Five, the penguins, the Cape endemics. They are
  seeded for you, and unlike before, **they are yours to change**: rename them, add to them,
  delete them.
- **Every list is a filter.** Tap a list's chip in the results strip and the table narrows to it.
  A site's list appears once you focus that site.
- **＋ list** freezes whatever is on screen into a new list. Filter to *Kruger · late July · birds*,
  tap ＋ list, and you have a targets list.
- **Sort → my lists** puts everything you have curated at the top: focal first, then tour, then
  anything else you have listed.
- **Manage them in section 08** — filter, rename, or delete any list.

**Sharing (this is the useful bit).** **Export lists ◆** writes a file with your lists and
**none of your field notes**. Hand it to someone; they **Import…** it and your lists are *added*
to theirs — nothing of theirs is overwritten. Your notes stay yours.

---

## 6. The field journal (section 08)

A Grinnell-style journal, one page per site-day.

- **Journal** — free-text: what happened, conditions, who you met.
- **Species accounts** — anything you write a note about is promoted to an account.
- **The day's checklist** — tick a cell in the results matrix to log it here; tap a row to add
  a field note; **add a species not on the list** (matched to GBIF online, saved as a stub
  offline to resolve later).
- **eBird checklist** — paste your eBird checklist URL for the day.
- **← Back to explorer** stays pinned at the top (clears the Dynamic Island).
- **iNaturalist observation** — paste the URL (or just the observation number) on a species'
  page, and it now shows up **on that species' account and in the day's checklist**, as a link
  you can tap.
- **Export / Print** — **Export notes (JSON)** backs up the whole notebook (and re-imports
  later); **Print** each day for a clean PDF.

---

## 6b. Back up your notes — every day. This matters most.

Your notes, ticks and journal live **on that phone only**. Nothing is on a server, and a phone
can lose browser data. **They are the one thing here that cannot be rebuilt.**

- At the end of each day, open **section 08** and tap **Export notes (JSON)**. Save the file
  somewhere real — Files, iCloud, or email it to yourself.
- The app watches for this: when you have written anything since your last export, a warning
  appears next to the button — **"⚠ unsaved changes"** — with a **Back up now** button. When it
  is gone, you are backed up.
- **Export notes** = your notebook (private). **Export lists** = your picks (shareable). They are
  deliberately different files.

## 7. Comfort

- **◐ (top-right)** — light / **dark** mode. Dark is easy on the eyes in a dim cabin; the
  species list becomes light text on a dark background. Your choice is remembered.
- **A+ (top-right)** — text size, three steps.
